OPAL for Humanitarian Action

Devastating floods continue to ravage West and Central Africa, leaving a staggering humanitarian crisis in their wake. In recent weeks, over 1,000 people have lost their lives, and nearly one million have been displaced due to extreme flooding across Nigeria, Chad, Mali, Niger, and Senegal. Entire families have been left without food, shelter, or access to basic services as floodwaters destroyed homes, submerged hospitals, and wiped out infrastructure.

The impact of these floods is particularly severe in low- and middle-income countries, where nearly 90% of those highly affected already battle poverty. Women, children, the elderly, and people with disabilities are disproportionately affected by such crises. In Maiduguri, Nigeria, and across Senegal, the floods have compounded existing challenges of poverty, conflict, and displacement, affecting millions of people.

While Africa contributes only a small percentage of global greenhouse gas emissions, it is disproportionately affected by climate change. Rising temperatures and more frequent extreme weather events threaten to plunge millions further into poverty and hunger. The economic toll is also severe, with Senegal potentially losing up to 8% of its GDP by 2030 due to climate-induced disasters, according to the World Bank.

Without urgent action, the humanitarian and economic impacts will only grow. Costs are expected to reach $30 to $50 billion annually over the next decade across the affected regions. These floods expose the urgent need for climate adaptation measures and support for vulnerable communities in the face of escalating climate change impacts.
