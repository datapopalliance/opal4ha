Humanitarian Needs

In Senegal, addressing the flood crisis requires collaboration between government entities, international donors, civil society, and local communities. However, there are three significant challenges impeding effective flood response:

Lack of Timely, Accurate Data

Responding to floods demands precise data which is often unavailable in real time. Local and international actors are forced to rely on outdated and macro-level information, slowing down the response and leaving communities vulnerable.

Our solution focuses on leveraging cloud services like AWS to collect, process, and visualize high-resolution, real-time flood data. This will allow stakeholders to act faster, assess damage more accurately, and provide critical support where it's most needed.

Skills Gap in Utilizing Innovative Data Sources

While new technologies like Artificial Intelligence, Satellite Imagery, and Machine Learning are revolutionizing disaster management, local governments and humanitarian organizations often lack the capacity to use these tools effectively.

We propose training programs to build the necessary skills for local actors. By democratizing access to cutting-edge tools, we empower Senegalese institutions to better predict, prepare for, and respond to floods.

Communication and Coordination Barriers

Coordination among different actors is critical in disaster response, yet Senegal’s flood response efforts are hindered by fragmented communication and siloed operations. This leads to duplicated efforts, misinformation, and ineffective use of resources.

Our approach includes a centralized cloud-based platform that facilitates seamless communication between all actors involved in flood response. By providing a clear organizational chart, real-time data sharing, and coordinated actions, we can ensure a more efficient and impactful response to flood crises.
